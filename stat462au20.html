Student Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Demonstrate understanding of the probability foundations for stochastic processes through proofs and examples.
  • Build and use stochastic processes, including random walks, Markov chains, branching processes, Poisson and renewal processes.
  • Build and use continuous time stochastic process to model real-world phenomena.

Topics Covered

Discrete and continuous time stochastic processes with applications to physics, biology, actuarial science, and quantitative finance. Topics include: discrete- and continuous-time Markov chains, random walk, simple autoregression, branching processes, Poisson process, birth-and-death processes, renewal processes, Brownian motion and stochastic calculus.  We will use R for simple simulation problems: Professor Paul Hurtado's R resources.

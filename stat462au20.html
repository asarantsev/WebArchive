Student Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Demonstrate understanding of the probability foundations for stochastic processes through proofs and examples.
  • Build and use stochastic processes, including random walks, Markov chains, branching processes, Poisson and renewal processes.
  • Implement code in R or Python for real-world problems.

Topics Covered

Discrete and continuous time stochastic processes with applications to physics, biology, actuarial science, and quantitative finance. Topics include: discrete- and continuous-time Markov chains, random walk, simple autoregression, branching processes, Poisson process, birth-and-death processes, renewal processes, Brownian motion and stochastic calculus.  We will use R for simple simulation problems: Professor Paul Hurtado's R resources.

Grading Scheme

You are allowed to drop the lowest homework. Each homework is worth 6.5%, extra credit is worth 7%, and the final exam is worth 22%. This class is not a competitive environment. I wish every one of you succeeds. Therefore, your grade depends on your own total score, calculated as above. As an example, getting your total score above 90% guarantees you an A, regardless of what the rest of the class did. Relative comparison of students, the curve will be used to push grades up, not down.

  • B-: next 10% or total score above 74%
  • C+: total score above 70%

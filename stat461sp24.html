Textbook: Larsen & Marx, An Introduction to Mathematical Statistics, any edition. We shall cover Chapters 2-4. You might also find useful my Lecture Notes in Probability Theory for Undergraduate Students, sections 1-10. Some problems are taken from the Society of Actuaries Exam P Sample Problems.

Topics: Experiments, counting techniques, probability axioms; random variables, expectation, univariate and multivariate distribution theory, measures of association, moment generating functions, marginal and conditional probabilities, cumulative distribution functions, Bayes theorem, sequences of random variables, Chebyshev's inequality, Law of Large Numbers, and Central Limit Theorem.

Assessments: There are 12 homework assignments and 12 quizzes. Each homework must be submitted in person, written or typed on paper, NOT via Web Campus. Each quiz is 15 minutes at the end of the lecture, two problems. No cheat sheet, closed book, only calculator is OK. The midterm (March 20) is 5 problems, and the final exam (May 13, 8:00-10:00) is 10 problems, can use two-sided handwritten letter-size cheat sheet and a calculator. Any calculator model is OK. 

Grading: Each quiz is 2%, each homework is 3%, the midterm is 15%, and the final exam is 30%. You can drop the lowest quiz and the lowest HW. Class letter grade is given by: A 93%, A- 90%, B+ 85%, B 80%, B- 75%, C 70%, D 60%, F otherwise. The score is NOT rounded (89.9% means B+ not A-).

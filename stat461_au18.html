Student Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:
  • demonstrate understanding of randomness
  • use probability models to explain simple random phenomena
  • compute summaries of probability distributions (univariate and multivariate)
  • compute measures of location, dispersion, and association,
  • find probability of interest for many univariate and multivariate distributions
  • assess and use asymptotic results provided by the Law of Large Numbers and the Central Limit Theorem
  • and use them to estimate quantities from data

Syllabus

Experiments, counting techniques, probability axioms; random variables, expectation, univariate and multivariate distribution theory, measures of association, conditional probability, expectation, and distribution, Bayes' theorem, sequences of random variables, Chebyshev's inequality, Law of Large Numbers, and Central Limit Theorem.

Course Outline

Sections are from lecture notes.
  • Combinatorics (Section 1, Week 1)

  • Continuous Random Variables (Section 5, Weeks 6-7)

  • Probabilistic Inequalities and the Law of Large Numbers (Section 9, Week 11)
  • Central Limit Theorem (Section 6, Week 12)
  • Moment Generating Functions (Section 8, Weeks 13-14)

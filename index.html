Message to prospective MS/PhD students in Mathematics and Statistics

Thank you for your interest in applying for a graduate program in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics, University of Nevada, Reno. We do need strong and qualified graduate students. Admission decisions are made by a committee of professors. An individual professor cannot make such decision. The correct way to apply is go to this web site and follow the instructions. Admission deadline for Fall 2024 with financial support is February 1. After this date, a decision on whether to accept or reject your application will be made by a committee. Financial support will most likely include Teaching Assistantship, tuition waiver (free tuition), and health insurance. Emailing me and attaching your CV/Statement/Resume will not increase your chances of admission. Do not ask me questions about admission which can be answered by looking at the University web site -- this is disrespectful. Currently, I do not have any grants or other forms of financial support for you. I cannot give Research Assistantships to students. Usually a MS student chooses an adviser in the second year, and a PhD student in the second or third year of studies. Before that, students take classes required by this program.

Starting from July 2018, I am a tenure-track Assistant Professor at the Department of Mathematics and Statistics, University of Nevada, Reno. Research interests: Probability Theory, Statistics and Data Analysis, Stochastic Analysis, Stochastic Finance.

From Autumn 2015 to Summer 2018, I was a Visiting Assistant Professor at the Department of Statistics and Applied Probability, University of California, Santa Barbara. My postdoctoral mentor was Jean-Pierre Fouque, who partially supported me with his NSF grant DMS 1409434.

I got my Ph.D. in June 2015 from the Department of Mathematics, University of Washington, Seattle, under the supervision of Soumik Pal.
